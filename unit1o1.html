1.7 - Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

The General Data Protection Regulation 14 April 2016 (GDPR) is a legal framework that protects data and private information, meaning that all software should be written to comply with local and global requirements. This means that all codes should be written to secure private or senetive information, whether it be credit card details, home adress, phone numbers, bank details and so on. Companies have had to therefore update their policies to make sure that they the GDPR requirements so customers are assured that their information is secure and is updated or taken off if there is a time limit on.

Failing to follow these guidelines can cause seriuos problems and get a lot of compnaies into trouble if customers private information is leaked which could potentially end up in anyone's hand for misuse causeing the customer and the company a lot of serious damage and fraud. This would leave a bad impression on companies, negative feedbacks causing companies huge losses and huge fines. Not keeping within the guidelines impacts digital projects as it could affect their future developments and maintenance. For more information.
